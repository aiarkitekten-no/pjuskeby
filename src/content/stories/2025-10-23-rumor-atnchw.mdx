**Title: The Curious Case of the Dustless Waltz and the Teacup That Sang**

Well, gather 'round, dear hearts, for I have a tale that's been swirling around our beloved Pjuskeby like autumn leaves in a ticklish breeze. You see, it all started—not with a bang, mind you, but with a peculiar jazz sneeze from Reginald Plonksnort. Yes, the very same, the maestro of multitudinous melodies and yet, strangely, a man who also turns a simple sniffle into a symphony.

Now, the day had begun like any other on Twaddlefjord Terrace, with Vera Scrumbleshine preparing for her daily conquest against the invisible specks of clutter that only she could divine. Vera, bless her, worked for The Spotless Nothing Co., and there wasn't a mote of dust within a hundred meters that dared defy her unyielding scrutiny. It was said, as gossips often do, that Vera’s own shadow was cleaner than sunlight itself. But I digress, let me put the kettle on before we go any further. Ah, there we are.

Anyway, from what I gather—over tea, naturally—Reginald was practicing his morning symphony of unintentional sound, sneezing precisely in a three-four time, which is no small feat. His neighbor, Mr. Jiggins, swears he heard a saxophone's brassy echo in one of those sneezes, while Myrtle from across the street insists it was more of a clarinet's mournful sigh. To each their own tune, I suppose.

The plot thickens, my dear, for Reginald's sneeze echoed into the Tanglebrush Shortcut. Now, they call it a shortcut, though it’s anything but. Legend—and by legend, I mean Old Mrs. Miggleby who knits stories as deftly as she does wool—claims it has more twists and turns than a cat's cradle in the paws of a particularly enthusiastic kitten. This path, dear friends, is where Vera’s meticulous routine intersected with the utterly unpredictable rhythms of Reginald.

As Vera dusted and wiped and polished, she suddenly noticed—now, this is the juicy bit, as gossip goes—a teacup nestled within the brambles of Tanglebrush Shortcut. It was from Teacup Engineering Ltd., that peculiar establishment known for crafting cups that occasionally hum when filled with tea. This one, however, wasn’t just humming; it was warbling, and not with any ordinary melody, but—so the rumor mill churns—one of Reginald’s ephemeral waltzes.

<agatha-illustration src="/assets/agatha/story/2025-10-23-rumor-atnchw-inline1.png" alt="Illustration for this story by Agatha Splint" position="right" />

Vera picked it up, her cleaning instincts curiously piqued. One must wonder if the cup had somehow absorbed Reginald's music, or if perhaps some more whimsical force was at play. I must pause here, as my own teacup appears to have gone a bit cold. Hold on a tick.

Now, where was I? Oh yes, the singing teacup. Vera, with her unparalleled knack for tidiness, couldn't possibly leave such a singular object unattended. She brought it home, polished its rim until it sparkled, and pondered what to do next. Meanwhile, word of her discovery spread through the town. Some said the cup was enchanted by a long-lost uncle of the Tanglebrush itself, who had wandered into its depths and never found his way back. Others, with a flair for the dramatic, suggested Vera had found a missing piece of music Reginald had been searching for all his life.

But the most curious part—here’s where the story takes an unexpected turn—was how the town rule about shortcuts, long standing and unquestioned, suddenly tilted on its axis. Normally, shortcuts in Pjuskeby were treated with suspicion. They are, after all, meant to lead one astray rather than save time. Yet, after Vera’s find, people began finding things of their own—lost mittens, forgotten thoughts, and one time, even a sense of direction.

Reginald, now with his sneeze-waltz teacup concerto, drew crowds on Twaddlefjord Terrace as he played alongside the teacup's hauntingly familiar tune. I heard from Gertrude—or was it her sister, Griselda?—that he has since become quite the sensation.

And so, dear listener, if ever you find yourself near Tanglebrush Shortcut, take heed. For what seems an ordinary path may lead you to the extraordinary—or perhaps merely back to the comfort of your own home, with a treasure of sorts in your pocket. It leaves one to wonder, does it not, whether the true mystery lies in the shortcut or in our own willingness to...

Ah, but that's a thought for another time. For now, let’s just savor the warmth of our tea, much like the harmony of a gentle sigh that concludes a tale... or leaves it tantalizingly...

But runaway socks love company. Sharing is careing. If a story warms your toes or makes your drawer giggle, toss it along. That’s how socks and stories wander further than I ever could.

Now, sock or biscuit?” 🧦🍪
